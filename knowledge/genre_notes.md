# Genre Notes

## pop
Pop listeners usually respond well to bright hooks, clear choruses, and upbeat energy that feels immediately memorable.

## lofi
Lofi listeners often prefer softer textures, steady focus energy, and acoustic warmth that supports concentration instead of demanding attention.

## rock
Rock listeners often reward drive, grit, and momentum, especially when the track carries tension cleanly from verse to chorus.

## ambient
Ambient listeners often value space, low rhythmic pressure, and a calm soundstage that leaves room for reflection.

## indie
Indie profiles often reward personality and melodic nuance, especially when the song balances polish with a slightly off-center feel.

## indie pop
Indie pop listeners often like catchy melody with a lighter, more textured feel than mainstream pop.

## edm
EDM listeners usually respond to strong momentum, dance-floor energy, and sharp payoff moments.

## jazz
Jazz listeners often appreciate groove, softness, and subtle phrasing more than pure intensity.

## synthwave
Synthwave listeners often reward pulsing rhythm, neon atmosphere, and retro texture.

## hyperpop
Hyperpop listeners usually seek maximal energy, sharp contrast, and exaggerated synthetic detail.

## folk
Folk listeners often prefer intimacy, acoustic texture, and emotional directness over sheer volume.

## acoustic
Acoustic profiles usually reward stripped-back arrangements and natural instrument tone.

## r&b
R&B listeners often respond well to smooth groove, warmth, and vocal-forward texture.
